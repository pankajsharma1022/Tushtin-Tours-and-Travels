Andhra Pradesh is famous for its cosmopolitan nature and various customs and culture prevalent in the state. Many festivals of different communities are celebrated here. Most of these festivals are organized by the Andhra Pradesh state government. The festivals here showcase the art and culture here. The festival of Pongal is mainly celebrated in Andhra Pradesh. Which symbolizes the harvesting of crops. Shivaratri is one of the oldest festivals celebrated in Andhra Pradesh. Other popular festivals of Andhra Pradesh are Ugadi, Holi, Ganesh Chaturthi, Diwali, Deccan festival, Lumbini festival and Rayalaseema food and dance festival being the main ones.

The festival of Lumbini signifies the importance of Buddhism as a famous religion in the state of Andhra Pradesh. Lumbini festival is celebrated every year in Andhra Pradesh to revive the Buddhist heritage in the state. Gautam Buddha was born 563 years before Christ in Lumbini forest of Nepal on the full moon day of Shukla Paksha of Vaishakh month. The Lumbini festival usually starts on the second Friday in the month of December and lasts for three days.

The festival of Pongal is dedicated to the gods related to agriculture and harvest. The name of this festival is Pongal. Because the prasad that is offered to the Sun God on this day is called Pogal. Another meaning of Pongal emerges in Tamil language. "boil well" If seen in both the forms, then one thing comes out that it is boiled well and offered prasad to the Sun God. Praying to God for a good harvest. It is on these days that the painting of houses starts. On the new moon day everyone gathers at one place. On this occasion people find solutions to their problems.

The festival of Brahmotsavam is one of the major annual festivals celebrated at Tirumala Tirupati Temple. Celebrated for nine days, this festival is dedicated to Lord Venkateswara. This festival is organized in a grand manner. During this festival, devotees from all over the country come to see Lord Venkateswara. It is believed that whoever witnesses the bathing rituals of Lord Venkateswara, He attains salvation. This is the reason why Vaishnava devotees of the country as well as devotees from abroad also come in large numbers on this festival.

On the day of Ganesh ChaturthiGanpati Bappa Morya, the whole country resonates with these cheers. In every shop, every house and every street, only Bappa is Bappa. It seems as if God himself has come down to earth. In Shiva Purana, the Chaturthi of Krishna Paksha of Bhadrapada month has been described as the date of incarnation of Mangalmurti Ganesha. Whereas, according to the Ganesha Purana, this Ganeshavatar happened on Bhadrapada Shukla Chaturthi. Gana Pati-Ganapati. According to the Sanskrit dictionary 'Gana' means holy. 'Pati' means lord, 'Ganapati' means lord of the holy ones.

Vishakh Utsav is another festival which is organized by Andhra Pradesh Tourism Development Corporation, So that tourism can be promoted in the state. This festival is the most special event, Which celebrates traditions and culture in the form of arts, crafts, music, dance and many cultural events.
