The Indian state of Bihar has its own art and culture. Bihar is a background in the history of India. The culture, civilization of India's ancient times are associated with Bihar. Bihar has a rich tradition of festivals since ancient times. The land of Bihar has a long list of ancient form festivals and fairs. which represents India. All the people mainly participate in the festival of Bihar. The festivals here bring each other closer. and show love. The most famous festival of Bihar, the city of faith and belief, is Chhath Puja in which Lord Surya is worshipped. This festival is celebrated twice a year. One on Shashti of Chaitra month and the other on Shashthi of Kartik month. Sama-Chakeva is another main festival of Bihar which is celebrated especially in Mithila. Other important festivals celebrated in Bihar are Ram Navami, Makar Sankranti, Nagpanchami, Bihula and Teej.

Buddha Purnima is also known as Vaishakh Purnima. It is the birth anniversary of Gautam Buddha and also his Nirvana Day. Lord Buddha attained enlightenment on this day. Today, more than 500 million people in the world who believe in Buddhism celebrate this day with great pomp. For Hindus, Buddha is the ninth incarnation of Vishnu. Therefore, this day is considered holy for Hindus also. For this reason, a place called Bodh Gaya in Bihar is a holy place of pilgrimage for Hindus and Buddhists. After leaving the house, Siddhartha wandered in the forest for seven years. Here he did severe penance and finally attained enlightenment under the Bodhi tree at Bodh Gaya on the day of Vaishakh Purnima. Since then this day is known as Buddha Purnima.

Chhath Puja is a festival of folk faith. This festival is celebrated twice a year. This festival is celebrated for the first time in Chaitra month and second time on the sixth day of Shukla Paksha of Kartik month. Chhath Mata is worshiped in this festival. Argha is offered to Lord Surya with raw milk and water. Apart from Bihar, Jharkhand, Uttar Pradesh in eastern India, Chhath festival is also celebrated in countries like Nepal and Mauritius. This is a four-day long festival. The main offering of Chhath is thekua made of wheat flour and jaggery. Women observe this festival for the longevity of husband and children. Men also observe Chhath fast for getting desired results and success in their work.

Madhushravani is a festival mainly prevalent in the Mithila region of Bihar. Suhagan women have deep faith in this fast. It is believed that by worshiping this fast, love and happiness remains in married life. The most enthusiasm and enthusiasm about Madhushravani Vrat is seen in newly married girls.

The initial inauguration of Rajgir Utsav was done by the then Chief Minister of Bihar Bindeshwari Dubey on April 4, 1986 in Swarna Bhadra area of ​​Rajgir. The festival was sponsored by the Tourism Department of the Government of Bihar after 1989. In 2013, Rajgir Mahotsav was organized from 25 to 27 November.

Sama-Chakeva is the festival of brother-sister love, during this folk festival starting from Kartik Shukla Paksha Saptami to full moon. Women collectively sing songs with statues of Sama Chakeva.
