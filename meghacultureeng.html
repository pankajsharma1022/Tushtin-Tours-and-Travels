The region is rich in tribal culture and folk traditions. Dancing and drinking, accompanied by vocal waves emanating from buffalo horns, flutes and mridangs, are an integral part of the social gatherings and religious rituals here. Marriage relations take place outside their clan-gotra. The advent of Christianity in the mid-19th century and the strict morality associated with it has damaged many tribal and community institutions. A strange practice among the Garo people is that after marriage, the youngest son-in-law comes to his mother-in-law's house and becomes his father-in-law's representative Nokrom in his mother-in-law's house. If the father-in-law dies, his widowed mother-in-law of Nokrom is married off (and this marriage is also granted the consummation of the marriage) and thus becomes the husband of both mother and daughter. This tradition is slowly dying out. Ritual killing was prevalent among Khasis in the past. The state of Meghalaya has many caves, mountain peaks, gardens, lake-resort sites, beautiful scenery, hot springs and waterfalls. The major tourist places are Shillong, Umiam, Cherrapunji, Mosinram, Jakriyam, Mairang, Jowai, Nartiang, Thadlashin, Tura, Siju and Balpakram National Parks. About 1,57,000 tourists visited the state in 1993.
