Situated in the heart of India, Chhattisgarh is known for its rich cultural heritage which reflects the various aspects of this beautiful state. Festivals in Chhattisgarh display the harmony and brotherhood among different communities. The major festivals celebrated in Chhattisgarh are- Goncha Festival, Bastar Dussehra, Kajari Festival, Madai Festival and Harli Festival. All these festivals provide an opportunity to the people here to celebrate. Through which people express their love towards each other. Many national festivals are also celebrated in Chhattisgarh.

Bastar Dussehra is one of the best traditional festivals organized in Bastar region of Chhattisgarh state. The festival of Bastar Dussehra is not related to the slaughter of Ravana of the epic Ramayana, but it is directly related to Mahishasur Mardini Maa Durga. The deities of other districts of the state are also invited in Bastar Dussehra.

Bhoramdev Festival was started 25 years ago in 1995. At this time Kawardha was a tehsil of Rajnandgaon district of undivided Madhya Pradesh. of forest The festival was organized to promote culture, art and folk color far and wide and to recognize Bhoramdev, the symbol of happiness of the forest dwellers.

Chakradhar Samorah is organized by the Cultural Department of the Government of Madhya Pradesh under the name of Maharaj Chakradhar. Maharaja Chakradhar, besides being a musician, was also a very skilled dancer. This is the reason why people coming from abroad and abroad Musicians and dancers perform here. If as a tourist you are also interested in Indian music, then this festival has a lot in store for you. From skilled sitar players, tabla players, singers, classical music singers, folk singers to Kathak, You find yourself in a different world in the company of poets who make beautiful combinations of words like Odissi and Bharatanatyam.

Goncha is the second important festival celebrated in public here along with the historical Dussehra of Bastar. Although Goncha has been celebrated as a festival of equality of all religions in the princely period and it was dominated by 360 Aranyak Samaj, which is still intact today.
