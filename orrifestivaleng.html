The state of Odisha in the Indian subcontinent is widely popular for its temple complex, tribal customs, spectacular beaches, fertile river valleys, striking waterfalls, lakes and wildlife sanctuaries. This state surprisingly combines antiquity and abundance. Hence the festival is celebrated in the state of Odisha with great enthusiasm and traditional fervor. Odisha participates in almost all the festivals that take place in the country. The festivals celebrated in Odisha symbolize the strong and holistic cultural heritage of our nation. Various communities of the Indian subcontinent celebrate forty festivals with full communal harmony. The festivals of Odisha are the best way to understand the multicolored culture of India. The festivals with all their colors and traditions also amalgamate together to give a true reflection of this state, which is so diverse in its wealth. You will experience the spirit of togetherness and social harmony in the festivals of Orissa.

Rath Yatra is celebrated in the month of June. In fact this festival of Puri is famous all over the world and is actually one of the famous festivals of Odisha. The festival can be traced back to the journey of Lord Jagannath and his siblings Subhadra and Balarama from the famous Jagannath temple to their maternal uncle's temple at Gundicha. They stay here for ten days and then return back to Jagannath temple with the same amount and show in ceremonial procedures. Thousands of devotees join the procession to pull the chariot. It is believed that pulling the chariot frees you from all sins and gives you virtue and salvation.

The festival of Durga Puja is celebrated as the victory of the Hindu goddess Durga over the evil demon Mahishasura. Therefore, the festival of Durga Puja is also considered as the victory of good over evil. It is not only the biggest Hindu festival but it is also the most important socio-culturally important festival in Bengali Hindu society.

Konark dance and music festival was established by Padmashree Guru Gangadhar Pradhan. Its main objective was to identify and develop Indian classical dance, which is one of the integral heritage of India, as these dance forms are prominently associated with temples.

Chandan Yatra: The tradition of sandal paste starting from the day of Baisakh Akshaya Tritiya has taken the form of Chandan Yatra festival in recent years. Sandalwood paste is applied to Lord Krishna all over the world. By giving the coolness of sandalwood to the Lord, devotees request him to remove their heat.

Shri Vinayaka Chaturthi celebrates the birthday of Lord Vinayaka or Lord Ganesha. It is generally celebrated on the 4th day of the second fortnight of Bhadrab month. During this festival clay idols are specially prepared by the people of Orissa and installed at home. Prayers are offered and hymns are sung in the honor of Lord Ganesha who is also known as the Lord of 'Vidya' and 'Sidhi' and is mainly worshiped by the student folk during the festival. The festival culminates with the immersion and immersion of the Lord in the waters of the nearby sea or river in the state.

Holi celebration Odisha is almost identical to the whole of West Bengal except in some minor aspects in the state and is certainly one of the popular festivals of Orissa. Another name of Holi here is 'Dol Purnima'. But instead of worshiping the idols of Lord Krishna and Radha on this auspicious occasion they worship the idol of Lord Jagannath, who is also considered as another incarnation of Lord Krishna. Holika is lit in the evening to mark the victory of good over evil.
