This state is a multi-religious, multilingual as well as multicultural state. Tribal people also live in Himachal Pradesh. Himachal is mainly known for handicrafts. Carpets, leather work, shawls, Kangra paintings, chambrumal, metal objects, wooden workmanship and paintings are the main examples of handicrafts here. Pashmina shawls of Himachal Pradesh are in demand not only in Himachal Pradesh but across the country. Himachali caps are mainly known for their workmanship. Local music and dance form the cultural identity of the state. They pray to God through dance and music on local festivals and special festivals. Apart from the fairs and festivals, many other festivals are also celebrated here with great pomp. Fairs are organized at various places in the region. Himachal Pradesh Religion The main religion of Himachal Pradesh is Hindu. More than 95% of the total population follow the Hindu religion. Himachal Pradesh has the highest proportion of Hindus as compared to other states and union territories in India. People of other religions living in Himachal Pradesh mainly include people of Islam, Buddhism and Sikhism. Muslims are mainly found in Una, Chamba, Sirmaur and Solan districts. The food of Himachal Pradesh is similar to that of most of the North Indians. They also have lentils, broth, rice, vegetables and bread. In comparison to other states of North India, non-vegetarian food is given priority here too. Special foods of Himachal Pradesh include Mahani, Patir, Madhra, Bhajeri, Chauchak, and Til ki Chutney.
