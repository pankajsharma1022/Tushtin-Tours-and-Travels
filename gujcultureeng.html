Most of the folk culture and folklore of Gujarat is reflected in the legends associated with Lord Krishna mentioned in the Hindu religious literature Puranas. Raas dance and Rasleela performed in honor of Krishna is still prevalent in the form of the famous folk dance "Garba". This dance is performed during the Navratri festival of Goddess Durga. A folk drama Bhavai is still in existence. Vaishnavism has flourished in Gujarat for a long time along with Shaivism, from which Bhakti school originated. Prominent saints, poets and musicians include Narsi Mehta, author of verses in the 15th century, a 16th-century man who abandoned his palace. Garba Dance, Gujarat Meerabai, a Rajput princess and author of hymns, Premanand, an 18th-century poet and writer, and Dayaram, the lyricist who popularized Bhakti school. Compared to other places in India, Jainism, characterized by non-violence and vegetarianism, is deeply rooted in Gujarat. The Zoroastrians, the followers of Zarathustra, first settled on the coast of Gujarat after fleeing Persia sometime after the 17th century. rural woman in traditional dress Most of the people of this community later migrated to Bombay (present-day Mumbai). Personalities like Krishna, Dayanand Saraswati, Mahatma Gandhi, Sardar Patel and famous cricketer Ranji made the society proud of the state. In the culture of Gujarat, mainly glass work and 'Garba' and 'Ras' dance are famous all over India. The most prominent folk dance of the state is Garba and Dandiya. In Garba dance, women dance with a perforated vessel on their head, inside which a lamp is lit. Men often participate in Dandiya but sometimes both men and women do it together. A great influence of Rajasthan can be seen on the lifestyle and dress of the state. Bhavai Loknatya is very popular in the state. The state is very rich in terms of architectural crafts. From this point of view, Rudra Mahalaya, Sidhpur, Matrimurti Pavagadh, Shilgaurav Galteshwar, Dwarkanath temple, Jain temple of Shatrunjay Palitana, Jalis of Sidi Syed Mosque, woodwork of Patan etc. are very important. The place which is of Surdas in Hindi is the same place in Gujarati as that of Narsi Mehta. Festivals and Fairs Gujarat, a prosperous state situated in the western part of India, is world famous for its festivals and cultural festivals. In the Shukla Paksha of Bhadrapadra (August-September) month, on Chaturthi, Panchami and Shashti, a Tarnetar fair is organized in the village of Tarnetar in praise of Lord Shiva. Madhavarai fair is held at Madhavpur near Porbandar on the Navami of Shukla Paksha of Chaitra (March-April) to commemorate Lord Krishna's marriage to Rukmani. Amba Ji Mela dedicated to Mother Amba is organized every year in Banskantha district of North Gujarat. The biggest annual fair of the state is organized in Dwarka and Dakor on the occasion of Janmashtami, the birthday of Lord Krishna, with great enthusiasm. Apart from this, Makar Sankranti, Navratri, Dangi Darbar, Shamla Ji Fair and Bhavnath Fair are also organized in Gujarat. colorful festival of kites Kite The kite festival celebrated on the festival of Makar Sankranti is very popular in the state of Gujarat due to its colorful image and has made a unique identity not only in India but also abroad. Uttarayan festival comes in the middle of January (January 14). Lord Bhaskar departs for Uttarayan, the spring season is coming, the happiness of the farmers knows no bounds because the crop is ready to be harvested, the time of harvesting comes. The luscious confectionery made from new grains gives the joy of celebration to all. Gujarat has a unique color of celebrating this festival. The people of Gujarat fill the sky with colorful kites. These colors symbolize unity in diversity, joy, enthusiasm and mutual affection and harmony. Kites are made from thin colored paper and bamboo fibres. The string of a kite is called a manjha, which is wrapped on a spin. The kite flyers first like the kite, whether it is properly built and balanced or not, then looking at the direction of the wind, let the kite fly high with skillful hands. Skilled fliers identify their opponent from the kites flying in the sky and then begin the tricks of kite cutting. Kite-cutters celebrate victory. It is such a colorful festival where children - old, rich - poor, indigenous - foreigner forget all the differences and fill the colors of unity in the infinite sky. In Gujarat, International Kite Festival is organized every year in Ahmedabad by the State Tourism Department since 1989. Guests from outside countries take part in this festival carrying different types of kites.
