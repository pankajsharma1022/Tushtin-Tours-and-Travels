The state of Tamil Nadu is considered a land of festivals. The festivals of Tamil Nadu are therefore a reflection of the rich Tamil culture and ethnicity. The fairs and festivals of Tamil Nadu are considered very striking. In fact it is a state where festivals are considered as a way of life. Enthusiasm, enthusiasm, fervor and festivities illuminate the entire state during the festival.

Pongal is the most important harvest festival celebrated by the people of Tamil Nadu. It lasts for 4 days and is usually celebrated from 13 to 16 January. This festival mainly shows gratitude to the Sun God for showering the land with abundant agriculture. People boil the first rice of the season as a mark of respect to the Sun God. Pongal is also the name of a dish which is very famous in the south and is cooked for most of the festivals there. The main Pongal falls on the second day and is called 'Thai Pongal'. Everyone wears traditional clothes. Gifts also include sugarcane and coconut.

Tourist Fair The exhibition introduces people to the cultural riches of Tamil Nadu, places of tourist interest, economic landscape and much more. Organized by Tamil Nadu Tourism Development Corporation, this fair organizes regular dance shows and food festivals to entertain the visitors.

Jali kattu or bull fight On the second day of Pongal i.e. Mattu Pongal, Jalli kattu or bull fight takes place in almost all villages. The youths come forward to retrieve the bull as they come running violently at them. It's a traditional, spine-tingling fight. The winner gets the prize money tied on the horns. Alanganallur near Madurai is famous for this sport.

Karthigai Deepam This festival is known as the 'Festival of Lights' in the state of Tamil Nadu and is celebrated with grandeur in the month of November and December or on the full moon day of the Tamil month of Karthigai. Being a very big and major festival of the state it is spread over a period of 9 days. It is also known as an extended Diwali and is one of the oldest as well as elaborate festivals of Tamil Nadu. A wide assortment of lit lamps in all shapes, sizes and colors adorn the temples and courtyards of homes. It is believed to ward off evils and usher in prosperity and happiness.

Chitrathi Mahotsava Brahmotsavam or Chitrathi festival is the main festival celebrated in all temples for 10 days. Every day 'Urchhavar' or procession Goddess is taken to different places like horse, bull, swan, lion, sun, moon etc. Lord Vishnu gets on a golden horse for the wedding of his sister Meenakshi in Madurai. . This parade is a spectacular sight.

Tamil New Year's Day also known as Vrishapirappu or Puthandu, Tamil New Year falls in the middle of the first month of the Tamil calendar. The dawn of Puthandu is marked as women make beautiful kolams at the entrance of their homes. On this day people wear new clothes and eat delicious food, one of which is 'mancha pachadi', a sweet and sour dish made of mango, jaggery and neem flowers.

Natyanjali Dance Festival This festival starts from the day of Mahashivratri and is celebrated for 5 days. This festival not only provides the best opportunity to the dancers to showcase their talent but also pays tribute to the 'Lord of the Lord, Shiva'. The festival has a special significance in Chidambaram and is jointly organized by the Department of Tourism, Government of Tamil Nadu, Ministry of Tourism, Government of India and The Natianjali Trust, Chidambaram. This dance festival is also designed to promote a universal message of 'Unity in Diversity'.
