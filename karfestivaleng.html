Karnataka is the land of festivals and festivals. The state of Karnataka has its own set of festivals, which have a special significance for the people of Karnataka. The festivals of Karnataka reveal the rich cultural features of Karnataka quite clearly. The state has a multi-religious population and many religious festivals are celebrated harmoniously. Apart from the religious festivals there are also some festivals which showcase the cultural wealth of the state, which again appear in myriad colors and forms. Then there is a host of festivals, which are associated with the lifestyle of the people and are celebrated with equal pomp and show.

Ganesh Chaturthi is the day when people celebrate in devotion to Lord Ganesha. The festival is celebrated over a period of 10 days. This waxing begins on the fourth day of the lunar period and ends on the 14th day in the month of August or September. The festival is celebrated in honor of the elephant-god Lord Ganesha, who is believed to be the remover of all obstacles and for prosperity and success. The main dessert of this festival is Modak which is a dumpling made from rice/wheat flour and stuffed with coconut, jaggery, dry fruits etc.

Hampi Festival is held in the first week of November and the people of Hampi, the capital of Vijayanagara, indulge themselves in a cheerful spirit with a collection of cultural performances. In this festival, Kannadigas show their talent in dance, art and music. There are also other activities like fireworks and puppetry which is an eye catcher for tourists everywhere.

Gauri Mahotsav is a festival celebrated a day before Ganesh Chaturthi as a day to honor Goddess Gauri, the mother of Lord Ganesha. It is celebrated not only in Karnataka but all over India to praise the goddess, so that strength and courage can be infused among the people.

The festival of Karga is celebrated at Darmaraya Temple in Bangalore. It is celebrated on the full moon day of Chaitra month, which is the first month according to the Hindu calendar. This popular festival is celebrated in the honor of Draupadi, the wife of the Pandavas, the famous characters of the great epic Mahabharata.

Ugadi is New Year's Day for Hindus of Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka and Telangana states of India.

Habba Habba means festival in Kannada terminology. Garden City is always full of lively people who love celebrations. Bengaluru Habba is a recurring event that lasts for 10 days every year. Artists from all over the world come together for this grand celebration.
