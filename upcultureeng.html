Uttar Pradesh is the cradle of ancient civilization of Hindus. Notable parts of the Vedic literature epics Ramayana and Mahabharata (which includes the Shrimad Bhagavad Gita) have their origins in several ashrams here. The texts and architecture of the Buddhist-Hindu period (about 600 BC-1200 AD) have contributed greatly to the Indian cultural heritage. The emblem of the Government of India since 1947 is based on the four lion-headed pillar (located at Sarnath near Varanasi) erected by the Mauryan emperor Ashoka. Architecture, painting, music, choreography and two languages ​​(Hindi and Urdu) flourished here during the Mughal period. The paintings of this period usually depict religious and historical texts. Although literature and music are mentioned in ancient Sanskrit texts and it is believed that music flourished in the Gupta period (around 320-540). Much of the musical tradition developed in Uttar Pradesh during this period. Musicians like Tansen and Baiju Bawra were in the court of Mughal emperor Akbar, who are famous in the state and the whole country even today. Two of the most famous instruments of Indian music, the sitar (stringed instrument of the veena family) and the tabla, developed in this region during this period. Kathak, a classical dance form developed as a devotional dance in the temples of Vrindavan and Mathura in Uttar Pradesh in the 18th century, is the most famous of the classical dance forms of northern India. Apart from this, there are also local songs and dances of rural areas. Most poplar of the folk songs are based on seasons. State Museum in Lucknow, Archaeological Museum in Mathura, Buddhist Archaeological Museum, Sarnath Museum are prominent among the art museums of Uttar Pradesh. The College of Art and Hindustani Music at Lucknow and Prayag Sangeet Samiti at Allahabad have contributed a lot to the development of art and classical music in the country. Nagri Pracharini Sabha, Hindi Sahitya Sammelan and Hindustani Akademi have been instrumental in the development of Hindi literature. Recently, the state government has established Urdu Academy for the protection and prosperity of Urdu literature.
