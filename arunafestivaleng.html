Arunachal Pradesh, the northeastern state of India, is famous for its beauty as well as its colorful festivals. The festivals here form an integral part of the socio-cultural life of the people of Arunachal. Most of their festivals are associated with agriculture. Because it is the main occupation of the people. Each tribe of Arunachal Pradesh celebrates its festivals throughout the year. Some of the major festivals of Arunachal Pradesh are Solang, Mopin, Losar, Bori Boot, Dri, Neechi Dau, Khan, Srastisowai, Loku, Longte Yullo, Moi, Nyokam, Ojilai, Reh, Sanken, Si-Donai and Tamladu.

The word Bori Boot means to unite irrespective of age, gender, caste for spring and successful harvest. The festival invokes the spirit of Bori Boot to bring them prosperity and free from any kind of diseases. Bori Boot is performed by Hill-Miris in the month of February.

New Year's Day is celebrated for five consecutive days in the state of Arunachal Pradesh in the form of Losar Festival. During this festival people involve themselves in cleaning their house to welcome the new and make use of the old. Prayers are offered for good health and prosperity during these five days. People also choose to hoist religious flags in their homes during these festivals. Buddhist scriptures are read in almost all homes and butter lamps are lit in almost all homes and premises.

Pangsau Pass is celebrated along the historic Stillwell Road in the Changlang district of Arunachal Pradesh. This great festival was first celebrated in the year 2007. Thousands of tourists from all over the Northeast and Myanmar come here to participate in this enchanting festival.

Solung is the main festival of the tribals. The festivities continue for five days. sowing seeds and transplanting paddy plants, To raise more mitts and pigs and to be free from natural calamities, fire, accidents, diseases etc. Solang is also celebrated to harvest a rich harvest.

Zero Music Festival is organized every year in Ziro for a week in the month of September. This festival has played a major role in enhancing the identity of Ziro, where thousands of music and art lovers from all over the country and abroad gather. It is noteworthy that in the year 2012, this festival was started by two artists named Bobby Hanno and Anoop Kutty. Which is emerging today in its fifth edition as an annual outdoor concert. If you want to dance forgetting everything in music, then definitely attend this festival once.
