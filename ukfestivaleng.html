Uttarakhand is one such state in India which is known as 'Devbhoomi' or the land of the gods. Let us tell you that Uttarakhand is a state which is full of many types of pilgrimage sites. Here people from all over India as well as India come here to join the spiritual and religious atmosphere. From the holy ghats of the Ganges in Haridwar, Uttarakhand to the snow-capped mountains of Auli, there are more than one holy place. The state of Uttarakhand has a diverse and vibrant culture. Because many ethnic groups, tribal communities and groups of immigrants also reside in this state.

Ganga Dussehra is celebrated on Dashami (tenth day) of Jyeshtha month. Let us tell you that this festival is a ten-day long festival. It is seen on the ghats of the Ganges at Haridwar, Rishikesh and Allahabad. There is such a belief behind celebrating this festival that on this day Mother Ganga descended on the earth. On this day a large number of devotees take a dip in the Ganges in the hope of getting rid of their sins.

Phool Dei is a festival that celebrates the coming of the year's harvest and spring season. Let us tell you that this festival takes place on the first day of Chaitra season (March-April) according to the two calendar. It is a major festival of Uttarakhand which is also known as harvest festival. On this special occasion a dish prepared using jaggery or jaggery, curd and flour is one of the most special part of this festival.

Uttarakhand is one such state in India where some festival is celebrated in every season. Let us tell you that Harela is a festival which marks the beginning of the rainy season or monsoon. People belonging to the Kumaon community organize this festival in the month of Shravan, that is, during July-August. Mythologically, this festival is celebrated to commemorate the marriage of Lord Shiva and Parvati.

Where women sow seeds in the soil and reap the harvest till the end of the festival. Brothers also give gifts to their sisters on this special occasion.

Kale Kawa is a major festival of Uttarakhand. Let us tell you that this festival is celebrated at the time of Makar Sankranti. On this festival the local people prepare sweets made from flour like knives, swords etc. During this time the local people welcome crows and migratory birds by feeding them and also hope that they come back next year also. Please tell that songs are also sung by the children to attract the migratory birds.
