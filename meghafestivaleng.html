Meghalaya literally means 'Land of the Clouds'. The state of Meghalaya is believed to be the home of dance and music. The festivals of Meghalaya are associated with their different dance forms and hence are enjoyed throughout the year. Each tribe has its own set of festivals in which they actively participate in order to enhance their festive spirit and come to terms with their monotonous life. In fact the indigenous festivals of this beautiful state reflect its diverse heritage during the celebration of the seasonal cycle of sowing and harvesting. Most of the festivals of Meghalaya revolve around agriculture, which is the main occupation of the people of the state. Although some religious and spiritual sentiments are intertwined in secular rites and rituals, the pre-eminent theme of the festival is the offering of prayers to the Supreme who is known by various names in various Naga dialects. But everyone's participation is mandatory in these festivals.

Behdinkhlam festival is the most famous festival celebrated by the Panar tribe of Meghalaya. This festival is one of the major festivals celebrated in Meghalaya which is celebrated for three days in a year. This cultural festival is celebrated in the town of Jowai, the headquarters of Jaintia Hills district in Meghalaya. This beautiful town is a small plateau surrounded by Jaintia hills. Behideenkhalam festival literally translated means Behideenkhalam to drive away evil spirits. It is a Hindu festival celebrated by the Panar tribe, the followers of the Nimtre tradition.

Ka Pamblang Nongkrem is one such festival which is celebrated to thank nature along with the joy of good harvest. During this time, various tribes and communities along with the local residents present different types of folk dances and folk music programs. Nongkrem is actually a famous folk dance of this state, which is performed to please the deities and get their blessings and wish for good harvest. This five-day long festival is celebrated in the month of November.

Shad Suk Mynasim festival is actually a thanksgiving ceremony celebrated during the spring season on the Khasi hills. Beautiful women dressed in traditional beauties and men-folk in attractive colorful costumes participate with great enthusiasm in the traditional dance to the accompaniment of drum beats and other musical instruments.

Shad Be Sear: This deer hunting dance festival is entirely dedicated to commercial pleasure and is one of the most cherished festivals of Meghalaya. During the off-harvest season, males roam the dense forests hunting deer. When one or two deer are killed it becomes a local celebration. The youth of the village stand the killed deer on a bamboo pier and parade through the villages. The very hunting adventure turns into an astonishing spectacle.

Doregata Dance Festival: This is a very popular dance festival organized in the state of Meghalaya. In this dance, the female members try to tie the turbans of their male counterparts. If the women are able to successfully beat the turban while dancing, it is followed by a plethora of laughs.
