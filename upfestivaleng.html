Uttar Pradesh is one of the top travel destinations in India where you can find all kinds of multi-colored festivals celebrated by people from all communities. These festivals are filled with messages depicting one or more aspects of human life, relationships or the earliest traditions. Many of these festivals like Diwali, Dussehra, Shivratri, etc. are celebrated across the country. The festivals of Uttar Pradesh can be redefined as the religious celebration of the soul. Since ancient times, the state of Uttar Pradesh has been attracting the whole world with its rich cultural heritage and spirituality. The festivals of almost all the religions are celebrated with equal grandeur and cheer in the state of Uttar Pradesh. No one can shy away from the simple yet alluring charm of the festive season of Uttar Pradesh. The festivals celebrated in Uttar Pradesh symbolize the strong and holistic cultural heritage of the nation. The timing of festivals is the ideal time to listen and watch the world's famous artists live; It will be a thrilling experience to listen to traditional music and folk songs in perfect ambiance.

Diwali is indeed the most special festival in this Hindu state and is celebrated with great zeal and enthusiasm. The state wears a vibrant color during the Diwali festival and almost seems alive with gusto. This festival is celebrated to commemorate the victory of Lord Rama over the demon king Ravana and his return home to Ayodhya after 14 years of exile.

Makar Sankranti is a Hindu festival. It falls in the month of December-January. People celebrate Makar Sankranti by bathing in the river and it is mandatory in Uttar Pradesh.

Maha Kumbh Mela is the major Hindu pilgrimage where lakhs of people gather together to take a religious dip in a holy river, commonly known as 'Sangam'. It is held every third year at one of the four locations of Haridwar, Prayagraj, Nashik and Ujjain.

Ram Navami Mela The holy city, a holy pilgrimage site for Hindus, hosts the Ram Navami Festival in the month of April. Thousands of devotees meet to worship the Lord at Kanak Bhavan.

Buddha Purnima The major day in the life of Buddha, Buddha Purnima is celebrated in the Buddhist world as the day of birth, enlightenment and death of Gautama Buddha. India, the land of Gautama Buddha's enlightenment and death, sees great celebration of his life and teachings by Buddhist temples and followers in the country. Spiritual Buddhists gather in their nearby temples carrying flowers and candle sticks, praying and singing hymns. Bodh Gaya in Bihar is witness to the grand celebration of Buddha Purnima in India.

The epic Ramayana is represented in Ramlila. It depicts the life and times of Lord Rama. Ramlila is associated with Vijayadashami and Ramnavami celebrations.
