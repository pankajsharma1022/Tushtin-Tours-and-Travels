Religion in Arunachal Pradesh Although the state is inhabited by people of all major religions, but the largest number of people of the total population of the state are Christian, Hindu and Buddhist. Apart from this, there is a sequence of people of religions like Muslims, Jains, Sikhs, etc., with the followers of the major religions, up to 26 percent of the total population is of other tribes and community. Major Languages ​​of Arunachal Pradesh – Languages ​​of Arunachal Pradesh The state has officially accepted the English language, under which most of the administrative work is completed by it. But due to the residence of different tribes in the state, everyone has their own independent languages, which include Nepali, Nyishi, Apatani, Mishmi, Chakma, Tangsa, Nocte, Wancho, Tagin, Bhotia etc. Along with this, among other Indian languages, Hindi, Bhojpuri, Bengali, Assamese, etc., are also commonly used here in dialect. Tradition and Culture of Arunachal Pradesh With about 26 tribes inhabiting the state, most of the tribal beliefs and traditions have a clear influence in the lifestyle here, the Monpa and Sherdukpen communities are considered as the original inhabitants of the state, which is of Mahayana Buddhism. used to follow. The influence of the Tibetan Lama Buddhist system is more on this community, according to which they are seen living even today. From the earliest times, there is a custom of having a chief head of this community, who enforces specific rules and writes down various incidents related to the community and keeps it alive for centuries. In the state, you will find some such tribes who consider the sun and moon as the supreme power and are adopting the worship system associated with it, mainly the Adi, Mijis, Bangnis, Nishis, Mishmi and Thongsas communities. Apart from this, you will also find a community of specific tribes who follow Vaishnavism and follow the beliefs, rituals, fasts, festivals related to it in the state. Special languages ​​of different communities are used here in common dialect, in which English language has got the first place here for education. The number of people of Christian, Hindu, Buddhist religion is more in the state, apart from this religious people like Jain, Sikh, Muslim etc. also live, due to this, festivals related to all these religions are celebrated throughout the year under the state. Overall, the people of Arunachal Pradesh live a mixed lifestyle, in which the influence of western style is seen nowadays. Music and Dance Art of Arunachal Pradesh - Music and Dance Art in Arunachal Pradesh The music here mostly includes folk songs, which include descriptions of activities related to social life, historical background, beliefs and practices. Traditional music like Ja-jin-ja and Nyoga etc. is mainly enjoyed here on occasions like wedding, apart from this, Barai music has become more prevalent during cultural or religious festivals, which is song music associated with historical background and beliefs. is. Talking about the traditional dance form, the famous music forms of the state include Roppi, Lion Dance, Rekham Pada, Hurkani, Aji Lamu, Ropir, Chalo, Ponung, Buiya, Peacock Dance and Pasi Kongki. Major food consumed in the state – Staple Food in Arunachal Pradesh Like other states of Northeast India, in Arunachal Pradesh, the main type of food included in the diet is rice, along with various dishes made from bamboo and other leafy vegetables are also commonly eaten. We have given the information below, which includes – 1. Adi community – A garment called gale is worn by both men and women of this tribe, in which men wear a crown made of deer, bear, etc. on their heads. The traditional dress type of women of this community is crinoline, which is like a blouse, apart from this, virgin girls also wear jewelery which is known as beyop. 2. Naga and Tangsa community – The distinctive identity of the people of this community is their attitude in which men wear green colored lungi and sleeveless shirt, the same blouse is generally worn by women. 3. Monpa – The women of this community generally wear sleeveless shirt and jacket, while men are wearing a neck-to-toe jacket type, in which the shirt is worn inside it.
