The festival celebrated in Mizoram gets to know about their culture and costumes. If you want to know more about Mizoram. So you can understand from the festival celebrated in Mizoram. The people of Mizoram celebrate festivals as mutual brotherhood. Which is a symbol of mutual love and consent. Mizoram has its own rich culture which is still alive today because of these festivals. Mizoram has a high population of Christian people. Hence the festival of Christmas is also celebrated.

Chapchar Koot festival is related to harvesting of agriculture, forest. This festival is celebrated during the spring. In this local festival, young men and women dance in the traditional nurturing. People come from far away to see it. Tribal people from all corners of the state gather to celebrate the festival. They participate in a procession called Koot Rore. And decorated girls dance to the tunes of the Boss. This dance is called Cherav Nritya in the traditional language here. During this, the boss's forests are cut down to make cultivable land. Then the cut bamboo is burnt. It is known as the festival of Chapchar Koot. The people of Mizoram have kept their art, dress and costumes alive even today through festivals. Such a government also insisted on saving the culture, dance music and art here. To promote the culture of Mizoram. Dance, music are also organized in Chapchar festival. Which works to promote the arts of Mizoram. Apart from this, one can also enjoy the traditional food here. Hand made artefacts can be seen in this festival. And can also buy. Chapchar Koot festival is the biggest festival of Mizoram.

Pal Koot festival is a harvest festival. Which the Mizo people celebrate with great pomp. Like other Mizoram festivals, the festival is also celebrated. But this festival is celebrated in the time of December. There are also some stories behind this festival. There was a huge drought in the 18th century. The people of Mizo tribe pray to God to end the drought. God listens to their prayer. And it rains heavily. After this the Mizo Longo promised to give their first crop. Which was a gift to God. Since then Prasad was kept, since then it is celebrated as Paul Koot festival. The Mizos go for hunting in the forest for this festival. Birds and animals are hunted for meat. In which pork is prepared for chicken dishes. After offering prasad to the Lord and offering prayers, they enjoy a delicious meal together. Which strengthens the mutual brotherhood. Local people enjoy dance, music in traditional dress. Mizo people display their own culture, art, food, dance.

Mim Koot Festival is an important festival for the Mizo people which is celebrated as a harvest festival. The Mim Koot festival is similar to other festivals celebrated in Mizoram. It shows the connection with the culture here. The festival celebrated after the harvest is celebrated to thank God and celebrate happiness. This festival enhances social and family love. Mizos enjoy delicious dishes during this festival. and share happiness with each other. And people of all religions live with mutual brotherhood. Local people organize dance music in their traditional dress. Due to which people coming from every corner of the country get acquainted with its local art, dance and music.

Anthuriam Festival is the most popular festival celebrated in the state today. It is a successful tourism promotion initiative of the Department of Tourism which lasts for three days and is celebrated every year at the Tourist Resort Reek located in Reek Village, a quaint and picturesque village about an hour's drive from the capital of Mizoram, Aizawl. It is celebrated as a festival amidst nature during the peak season of the beautiful and attractive Anthurium blossom. It is a three day festival of culture, music, dance, sports, fashion show, handloom and handicrafts exhibition and traditional cuisine. It is set in a kaleidoscope of colorful anthurium flowers against the majestic green backdrop of the Reek Mountains.

Winter Festival is an annual three-day festival organized by the Department of Tourism, Government of Mizoram. The venue for the celebration is set at Tuirial Airfield, an abandoned airstrip 21 km from the city centre. It is celebrated to showcase the diverse culture and traditions of Mizos along with a host of entertainments. It is usually celebrated during the second last weekend of November. The Winter Festival was originally known as Thalafwang Kut. The origins of Thalfwang Kut reflect the close unit society and deep friendship that weaves the Mizos together, wherever they are. In the 1960s and 70s, insurgency in Mizoram displaced families from their homes, villages and towns.
