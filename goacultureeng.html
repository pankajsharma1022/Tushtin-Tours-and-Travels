Culture And Tradition Of Goa State – Culture And Tradition Of Goa The culture of this state is mainly divided into Hindu and Catholic population. People respect both the cultures. Being connected by air and rail, it is also visited by people from neighboring states. New people who came from other states of India have also started living here. The people of Goa's Catholic religion respect Hindu culture and at the same time adopt Hindu customs. The love between the people of both the religions can be seen here. Temples of Hindu religion have also been built in many places in the state, where idols of the gods of Hindu religion have also been installed. In the pre-independence period, the English, Portuguese, Dutch people who came to India from the commercial point of view had settled in some specific places in India in the early days, in this Goa was the main center of business and residence of the Portuguese and Dutch people. The influence of mainly Christian, Hindu culture is more visible on Jovan style, although other religious people are also found under the state. Mainly churches, temples, mosques, gurudwaras are also present in the prayer places here, according to which festivals of all religions are also celebrated in the state. Goa has a special identity in the country and the world because of its beautiful tourist places, where tourists from different countries come here for centuries, so the influence of western culture is also seen here. Goa is also closely related to Konkani traditions, hence the festival of Coconut Purnima is also celebrated here. Overall a happy mood, the culture of Atithi Devo Bhava is seen in Goa, as the biggest economic source of this state depends on tourism. is. Music and Dance Art of Goa State – Music and Folk Dance in Goa State Goa is considered to be a favorite place of foreign tourists in India, where people from different countries come throughout the year, its influence is also seen on the music here, in which there is a lot of pop music, drums, hip hop, rap songs, Portuguese music etc. There has been a trend for which instruments like violin, guitar, trumpet, piano etc. are used. Along with Indian classical music, Konkani songs, Koli songs are also particularly liked here, where these songs are more liked by the fishermen community. Music for these songs is Mridang, Tabla, Dhol, Flute, Shehnai, Dholak etc. Musical instruments are used. Various tribes present in the Hindus also have some amount of domicile here such as Dhangar, Koli, Bhoi etc. Traditional music music of the tribe is generally heard and liked within the state. Talking about dance, the state includes Diwali dance, Goff, The Delo dance, Ghode modni, Mando, Dekhni dance, Dhangar dance, Tonymel, Morulem, Koredinho, Kunbi dance, Mussol Nach, Jagor, Bhonvado, Talgadi etc. Major Religions of Goa – Religion in Goa State People of Christian and Hindu religion are found in large numbers in the entire state, people of both these religions are domiciled in almost the whole of Goa, apart from this the number of religious people like Buddha, Sikh, Muslim, Parsi etc. can also say. Staple Food in Goa State Rice and fish curry is the staple diet of Goa. The cuisine of Goa is famous for its variety of fishes and spicy taste. Along with black pepper, spices and vinegar, most of the coconut and coconut oil are used in the food of Goa. The state of Goa is blessed with a rich natural coastline and tourists keep visiting the state throughout the year, due to which there is a great demand for Indian cuisine as well as continental food types, which include Chinese, Sri Lankan, Malaysian, The dishes of Portuguese, Brazil, European etc. countries are generally eaten. Along with Konkani food, the food of the provinces of Kerala, Diu, Daman, Malabar etc. is also found here, in which salads, pickles, curries, soups, fries etc. are prominently included. Fish, prawns, crabs, coconut dishes are eaten here at Sea Foods, in which rice and cashew are also the favorite food here. Among the meat types, chicken curry, chicken biryani, mutton curry etc. also include favorite dishes, among others. Tomato, pumpkin, orbjin, jackfruit etc. are used in vegetables. Cashew, papaya, pineapple, guava etc. are mainly used in fruit diet, alcohol is also consumed in large quantities in this state. Goa State Logo Costume – Dress/Costume of Goa State Peoples The main garment of women in the state is saree, in which Kunbi pallu is specially included here, like Maharashtra state, it is also the practice of some Marathi women to wear lugdi, in which blouse and lugdi and nostrils are worn in the nose. Navwari sarees are preferred among the sarees, apart from this a special dress called Pano Bhaju is also worn by the women here. In the men here, mainly cotton clothes are given more importance, in which shirts, T-shirts, plain pants and jeans etc. pants are generally worn. Coat suits etc. are also worn on occasions like weddings, etc. In this era of modernity, the trend of wearing mostly western clothes is more visible among men and women.
