The Tripuri culture owes its exaggeration to the tribal population, which includes tribes such as the Mussams, Chakmas, etc., each encompassing the entire personality and ethnicity of the cultural heritage. Poet Rabindranath Tagore's close association with Tripura has given shine to the rich cultural heritage of the state. All the tribes have their own different dance forms. Music and dance reflect the Tripuri culture and no body can deny it. Garia dance organized for the prosperity of the people; The 'Bizu' dance by the Chakmas, marks the end of the Bengali calendar year; The 'Hai Haq' dance of the Halams and the Cherou dance, associated with the imprisonment of the Lusai woman, are some illustrious examples. 'Basanta Raas' is a charismatic dance of Hindu 'Manipuris' in the state of Tripura. Tripura culture is very productive in the folk style of music. Singer Hemant Jamatia is a well-known name in the field of Indian music, who contributes to the nation with his melodious melodies and songs. Tripuris use melodious musical instruments, i.e. kham, made of wood and animal skin; sumui or flute made of bamboo, sarinda, chongpreng, dangu and cymbals are popular among the native Tripuris. Tripuri culture prides itself on the simple, yet creative and artistic excellence of the tribal communities of the region. Being a part of the North-Eastern region of India, it is known for its exotic mountain ranges, lush greenery and splendid flow of rivers. It has an abundance of strong flora and clear fauna. The tourism industry is on the rise to attract thousands of tourists and nature lovers from all over the country and it is home to thousands of tourists and nature lovers to enjoy the beauty of the region. Trilal Tripuri people are traditional and not adapted to the fast and modern lifestyle of the urban people. Pubs and discos are almost overlooked in the area. Excellent Variety of Handicrafts Using Bamboo and Cane: Wall mats, bamboo dining table mats, floor mats and many other gift items are in demand in the Indian market. Tourists can observe crafts persons working in different villages and buy handicrafts and handloom products from the state sales emporium and other private sales emporium undertakings, Purab, Tripura. Tripura culture is distinct from the rest of India because of its distinct indigenous cultural pride in the form of its music, dance, festivals, food and lifestyle of the Tripurans.
