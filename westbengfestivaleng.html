The festivals of West Bengal comprise the diverse cultures and traditions prevalent in this eastern Indian state. Different regional communities have different festivals according to their culture and traditions. Each festival is celebrated at different times with precise rituals, which are related to the festivals. There are some major festivals in this state, which are celebrated with grand arrangements; There are also some minor festivals which are celebrated with equal importance. Durga Puja, Nabo Borsho, Kali Puja, Jagadhatri Puja, Bhai Phanta, Saraswati Puja, Rath Yatra, Dol Purnima are the major festivals of West Bengal. These are celebrated with great ritualistic grandeur.

Nabo Borsho is the Bengali New Year of the Bengali community. It is celebrated in the month of 'Baisakh' or in the month of April. It is an extreme joyous occasion especially for Bengalis and businessmen. This is a great time to visit temples and relatives' homes, make prasad, buy new clothes, greet people and much more.

Dol Poornima: The festival of Holi is celebrated as 'Dol Utsav' in West Bengal. Among the many festivals of West Bengal, it is the most prominent. The festival of Holi in this state is famous by other names – 'Purna Purnima', 'Vasant Utsav' and 'Dol Yatra'. The festival was initiated in this state by the world famous poet Rabindranath Tagore at Visva Bharati University, of which he was a pioneer. The people of the state not only welcome the spring season with colors and sweets, but also bhajans and other devotional songs.

Rath Yatra: It is the birthday celebration of Lord Jagannath, who travels to his maternal uncle's house by his chariot on this day and returns after a week. The most famous Rath Yatra of West Bengal is the Rath Yatra of Mahesh in Serampore. It attracts lakhs of tourists from across the state as well as the country. The day is considered very auspicious and marks the beginning of the sowing season for the monsoon crop across eastern India. Grand fairs are organized all over the state, which are called 'Rather Mela'.

Janmashtami: Janmashtami is celebrated with great enthusiasm and fervor in West Bengal, which marks the birth of Lord Krishna's love. This festival of West Bengal falls on the Ashtami day in the month of Shravan according to the Hindu calendar. In Krishna temples 'Rasleela' is performed to recreate events from the life of Krishna and to celebrate his love for Radha.

Rakhi Purnima: It is one of the popular festivals of West Bengal and it signifies love and affection between brothers and sisters. On this special day sisters tie a colored bandage on the wrist of their brothers with the belief that it will bring peace, success and good health in their brother's life throughout the year. Brothers also take vows on their behalf to save their sister from all troubles. They also give some gifts to their sisters as a token of love.

Durga Puja: Durga Puja is the most important festival of West Bengal. This festival is celebrated in the months of September or October. Bengalis believe that with the arrival of Goddess Durga, there will be prosperity among all the people of West Bengal with the children going to her maternal home from Kailash. Durga Puja is celebrated here for five days – Saptami, Saptami, Ashtami, Navami and Dashami. It is believed that Goddess-Durga will return to Kailash again on Dashami after slaying the demon-Mahisasur. Hence, Goddess Durga is worshiped as 'Mahishasurmardini'.

Kali Puja: After Durga Puja, Kali Puja is one of the grand festivals of West Bengal. The worship of Goddess Kali makes the festival of Diwali very unique in the state of West Bengal. Homes and temples throughout the state are vibrantly decorated and lit with oil lamps, candles or 'diyas'. All family members gather in the evening to burst crackers together during Kali Puja. Goddess Kali is worshiped during 'Amavasya'. 'Bhoot Chaturdashi' is performed a day before Kali Puja, when each household in Bengali lights 14 diyas simultaneously and they also take 14 types of leafy vegetables. 'Bhoot Chaturdashi' is considered a powerful day for souls.

Jagdhatri Puja: This festival is celebrated in a very fun and adorable way in the Bengali month of Kartik or the English month of November. During this puja, Chandannagar in Hooghly district of the state is decorated with huge pandals to worship Goddess Jagadhatri; Other regions of West Bengal also celebrate this festival.

Saraswati Puja: This is one of the most devotional festivals of West Bengal, where Goddess Saraswati is worshipped. The worship of Goddess Saraswati begins with morning 'aarti' and chanting of various 'mantras'. This festival is celebrated in all schools and colleges and youth can be seen participating in the puja with great enthusiasm.

Christmas: Christmas celebrations in the 'City of Joy' continue till the New Year. During this time, parts of Kolkata, especially the Park Street area, are decorated with Christmas trees and lights. Kolkata also hosts the Winter Carnival at this time; People also come to church.
