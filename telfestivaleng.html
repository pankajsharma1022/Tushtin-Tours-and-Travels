Telangana is a land of beauty and cultural diversity. It is inhabited by different communities. Due to which diversity of all religions and castes lives here. Since different communities of different religions live here, many festivals are celebrated here throughout the year. Which give Telangana a colorful atmosphere. All the festivals here are celebrated with enthusiasm and enthusiasm. The festival of Bathakamma Panduga is mainly celebrated in Telangana. It is celebrated at the time of Dussehra. This festival begins with the new moon at the end of the rainy season. Other popular festivals of Telangana include Sadar festival, Ayak or Bhimanna, Holi, Ganesh Chaturthi, Diwali, Dussehra, Muharram and Ramzan.

Bonulu or Goddess Mahakali Bonulu is a Hindu festival in which Goddess Mahakali is worshipped. Bonalu, the annual festival of Telangana which is celebrated in Hyderabad, Secunderabad and Telangana apart from many other parts of India. It is celebrated in the month of Ashadha i.e. in July/August. Special pujas are performed for Yellamma on the first and last day of the festival. This festival is celebrated to thank the goddess for fulfillment of vows.

The month of Ramadan holds great significance for Muslim communities. This month is considered to be the month of uniting the soul with the divine. Muslim communities believe that fasting in this month brings immense blessings from Allah. Ramadan started in the year 2 Hijri and since then Zakat was also obliged on the servants of Allah. In the month of Ramadan, every fasting person is very special for Allah and God blesses him with blessings and mercy.

Dussehra means Vijayadashami, which means the day of victory. It was on this day that Lord Rama won victory by killing Ravana. Rama is considered a symbol of good and Ravana is a symbol of evil. The victory of Rama over Ravana is seen as the victory of good over evil. On this day Mata Durga also conquered Mahishasura. Ramlila is shown till 14 days before Dussehra, in which the life Leela of Lord Rama is shown on the stage. Ravana is killed on the last day and with this the Ramlila also ends.

Bathukam festival is a regional festival celebrated by women in the state of Telangana, India. In the entire Telangana region, this Bathukam festival is celebrated for nine days starting from the new moon day of Shalivahana Samvat. The shape of the Gopuram temple is made with seven layers of flowers. Bathukamma in Telugu means Mother Goddess is alive. On this day Bathukumma is worshiped as Mahagauri. This festival is celebrated to honor the woman. Similar to Bathukam, Telangana celebrates the Bodemma festival, which is a seven-day long festival of Gauri Puja, which is celebrated by unmarried girls.
