The cultural life of Haryana reflects the rhythm of the various opportunities in the agricultural economy of the state and is a storehouse of traditions and folktales from ancient India. Haryana has a distinct dialect and local idioms are prevalent in it. The local folk songs and dances reflect the cultural life of the state in their own charming way. These are full of oomph and are infused with the humorousness of the local culture. In the spring festival of Holi, full of fun, people celebrate each other by blowing gulal and pouring wet colors, no distinction of age or social status. Janmashtami, the birthday of Lord Krishna, has special religious significance in Haryana, as Kurukshetra was the battlefield where Krishna preached the Bhagavad Gita (a part of the Mahabharata) to the warrior Arjuna. Lakhs of devotees from all over the country come to Kurukshetra for the holy bath on the occasion of solar eclipse. There are many ancient pilgrimage centers in the state including Agroh (near Hisar) and Pehowa. Agroha is known as Agrasen, who is considered to be the principal ancestor or originator of the Agrawal community and its sub-castes. Therefore Agroha is the birthplace of the entire Agrawal community. This community, prominent among the merchant classes of India, now spread in the country. As a mark of respect to Agrasen's birthplace, the community established a medical school at Agroha a few years back. Pehowa, situated on the banks of the sacred river Saraswati (the goddess of knowledge and arts according to the Vedas), is considered an important sacred place for the ancestors' Shradh Pindadaan. Religious rituals are performed in Pehowa for the peace of the soul, both unnatural or natural. Fairs organized in the memory of various gods and saints are an important part of the culture of Haryana. Animal fairs are also organized at many places. The area is also known for good quality milch animals, especially buffaloes and farm animals and crossbreds.
