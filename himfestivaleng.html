People of Himachal Pradesh love their festivals very much. People here participate in all their festivals and fairs with enthusiasm and enthusiasm. Most of the festivals of Himachal Pradesh are associated with seasonal changes. A glimpse of the culture and customs here is seen in the fairs here. People from all strata of the society participate in these festivals. Tribal festivals also have their own identity. Among the popular festivals celebrated in Himachal, the Pori festival is celebrated with great pomp at the Triloknath temple in Triloknath. Also the festival of Kullu Dussehra is celebrated on the grounds of Dholpur in October. The festival of Holi is also celebrated prominently here.

Dungri Utsav is a festival celebrated with pomp in the month of May every year in Manali, Himachal. It is one of the biggest festivals here. This fair is organized every year from 14 to 16 May on the birth anniversary of Hidimba Devi. The view of Manali is very beautiful during this festival celebrated for three days.

Halda festival is considered one of the famous festivals celebrated in Himachal Pradesh. Along with this, this festival is celebrated on the auspicious occasion of Magha Purnima, Halda. Along with this, this festival is celebrated as one of the beautiful festivals in Himachal Pradesh, Diwali, like the festival of lights.

Dussehra of Kullu festival holds great importance from tradition, customs and historical point of view. The Dussehra of Kullu in Himachal Pradesh is celebrated in a different and unique way. Here this festival is called Dashami. And it begins on the tenth day of the month of Ashwin. The color of this festival starts increasing even more in the valley of Kullu from that day when Vijayadashami ends all over India.

According to traditions, this 15-day long Losar festival marks the beginning of the new year and is celebrated on the first day of the lunisolar Tibetan calendar. The festival of Losar is celebrated with traditional and religious fervor in the Ladakh region of Jammu and Kashmir.

Sajo festival: This is the most visited festival of Himachal Pradesh. Sajo is known to bid farewell to village deities. In this festival, The doors of the temples remain closed but the vehicles of Gods and Goddesses remain open. It is said that on this day, various deities go to heaven for a short sleep.
