The cultural region of Bihar shows a close relationship with the linguistic region. Maithili is the language of the ancient 'Mithila' (Videha, present Tirhut), in which the Brahminical way of life predominates. Maithili is the only dialect of Bihar, which has its own script (Tirhut) and rich literary history. Vidyapati, one of the oldest and most famous composers of Maithili, is known for his erotic and devotional songs. Literature There is hardly any written literature in Bhojpuri dialect, but its oral folk literature is abundant. The folk literature of Magadhi is also very rich. In modern Hindi and Urdu literature, the creators of the plain areas of Bihar also have a significant contribution. tribal culture Most tribal villages have a dance stage, a sacred grove (sarna) for the worship of the presiding deity by the village priest and a dormitory (dhumkuria) for the unmarried. Weekly Haats play an important role in the tribal economy. Tribal festivals (such as Sarhul), Vasantotsav (Sohari) and Sheetotsav (Mage festival) are festivals of gaiety and gaiety. The original tribal culture is changing rapidly due to Christianity, industrialization, new communication links, tribal welfare programs and community development schemes. ancient landmarks The plains of the state are associated with places of religious and cultural importance. Nalanda had the famous Nalanda Buddhist University in ancient times. Rajgir and its nearby ancient and modern temples and shrines are visited by devotees of many religions. It was in Pavapuri that Mahavira, the founder of Jainism, attained Mahanirvana (enlightenment or liberation from the endless cycle of rebirth). Gaya is an important Hindu pilgrimage center and near it is located Bodh Gaya, the sacred site of Buddhism, where Mahatma Buddha attained 'Bodhitva'. One of the oldest and largest cattle fairs in India is held every November in the Harihar area near Sonpur, north of Patna. The place of 'Holi' and 'Chhath' (worship of Sun mainly by women) is very important in many Hindu festivals of Bihar. folk drama or dance The folk dances or Natyas of Bihar are organized on various cultural festivals, auspicious programs etc. [1] The main folk dances are as follows- jogida dance strainer dance launda dance khodlin dance panwadiya dance Dhebia Dance jhijhiya dance Kariya chandelier dance Vidapat dance woodpecker dance
