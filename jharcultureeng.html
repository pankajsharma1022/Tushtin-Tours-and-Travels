The cultural regions of Jharkhand are associated with their respective linguistic regions. Hindi, Santhali, Munda, Ho, Kudukh, Maithili, Malto, Kurmali, Khortha and Urdu languages ​​are spoken here. Despite not having written literature of Bhojpuri dialect, it has remarkable oral folk literature. Magahi also has a rich folk tradition. Most of the tribal villages have a dance hall. Paika, Chhau, Jadur, Karma, Nachni, Natua, Agni, Chokra, Santhal, Jamda, Ghatwari, Mahta, Sohari, Luriseri are the folk dances of this place. Each village has its own sacred tree (sarna), where worship is offered by the village priest. Apart from this, there is also a dormitory for the unmarried. Weekly Haat plays an important role in the tribal economy. Tribal festivals (such as Sarhul), Vasantotsav (Sohri) and Sheetotsav (Magh Parab) are occasions of gaiety. Tribal culture is changing rapidly due to external influences, such as Christianity, industrialization, new communication links, tribal welfare programs and community development projects. There are many places of religious and cultural importance here. Jamshedpur has Dimna Lake and Dalma Wildlife Sanctuary. Jubilee Park, a replica of the famous Vrindavan park, is spread over an area of ​​225 acres of Jamshedpur. Netarhat is one of the famous popular tourist destinations of the state. The holy city of Deoghar is famous for its Vaidyanath temple. Various Hindu festivals include Holi and Chhath (primarily sun worship by women).
