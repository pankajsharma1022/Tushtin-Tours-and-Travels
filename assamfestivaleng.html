The state of Assam of India celebrates festivals of different religions and different tribes. Here the colorful festivals of all the tribes are celebrated with great enthusiasm and enthusiasm. The main festivals celebrated showcasing the art-culture and civilization of Assam. Bihu which is celebrated in three forms. Bohag Bihu or Rongali Bihu in April, Magh Bihu or Bhogali Bihu in January and Kati Bihu or Kangli Bihu in October-November. Other festivals celebrated in Assam are Majuli Festival, Elephant Festival, Tea Festival, Dehing Patkai Festival and Brahmaputra Festival.

Baishagu is one of the most popular seasonal festivals of Bodos of Assam. In Assamese language it is called Bihu. This Baisagu festival is celebrated in the early part of the first month of the Assamese and Bengali year (Bohag or the first week of the month of Baisakh).

Bihu or Bohag Bihu is an important festival of Assam. Rongali Bihu in Assam is derived from many traditions Such as- Burmese-China, Austro-Asiatic, Indo-Aryan- and are celebrated with great enthusiasm. The festival begins in mid-April and usually continues for a month. This is the traditional new year. It is difficult to know exactly the reason for celebrating Bihu. But being a harvest festival, Bihu is considered to be associated with all the three crops of the year. On this day people build a temporary camp with paddy straw on the banks of the river or in an open space, which is called Bhelaghar. Actually the word Bihu is also believed to be associated with the word Bishuva i.e. Scorpion.

Majuli festival is celebrated annually for four days in the month of November. The festival begins on 21 November and ends on 24 November every year. Many cultural programs, folk dances as well as stalls of local delicacies of the tribes of Majuli are also organized in the Majuli Festival.

Rasleela or Krishnalila consists of the activities of young and child Krishna. Raslilas are staged at various places. In which decorated Shri Krishna is shown expressing his love for Radha by keeping different forms. Seeing these raas-lilas, the audience feels as if they have actually reached the era of Shri Krishna.

Tea Festival The people of Assam celebrate in Assam as Assam is the largest tea producer in India, which is attended by tea connoisseurs and tea lovers from all over the country. Jorhat district in Assam is the place which is known for its beautiful lush green tea gardens and is also the site of the festival. The Assam Tea Festival is full of cultural performances, fun activities and of course tea. People remember this season for their hard work. While the visitors show their gratitude by appreciating the variety and variety of tea displayed in the festival.
