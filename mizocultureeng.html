Dance and music are fundamental elements in the cultural life of Mizos. Festivals here include festivals of Christianity and local agricultural festivals (in Mizo the festival is called Kut), such as Chapcharkut, Pal Kut and Mimkut. The North Eastern University campus is located in Aizawl. language Mizo and English are the main and official languages ​​here; The Roman script is used for the Mizo language as it does not have its own script. The exact origin of the word 'Mizo' is not known. The influence of British missionaries spread here in the 19th century and at this time most of the Mizo citizens follow Christianity. Mizo language has no script of its own. The missionaries adopted the Mizo language and the 'Roman script' for formal education. religion About 85.57 percent of the population now practice Christianity due to conversions that began in the 1890s (though most of the changes occurred in the 1920s and 1930s); Most of them are Protestant. There are also Hindu (5 percent) and Buddhist (7.83%) minorities in the state. The majority of the state's population professes Christianity, and most of them are Presbyterians and Baptists. The people of Chakma caste are Theravada Buddhists. The number of Hindus and Muslims in the state is almost negligible. life life The population of Mizoram is made up of a combination of different groups, commonly known as Mizos, in the local language meaning the inhabitants of the highlands. Tribes of the region, many of whom formerly hunted humans, include the Kuki, Pavi, Lakher and Chakma. They are classified as Tibeto-Burmese people and use several Tibeto-Burman dialects; Some tribes are so isolated that their dialect is not understood even by those living in the neighboring valley. The density of population is 42 persons per square km. Density decreases from north to south, as humidity and temperature increase in the south, making conditions unsuitable for habitation. Density also decreases from west to east. There are 22 towns and 699 villages in the region, of which 663 have access to electricity. About 95 percent of the population belongs to the Scheduled Tribes.
