Jharkhand is a land of diverse culture and religions. It is visited by hundreds of tourists every year. Jharkhand celebrates various festivals every year. Jharkhand is famous for its tribal culture. Many fairs and festivals are organized here. Chatura Mela of Jharkhand is one of the major festivals celebrated here. This fair is organized at the time of Durga Puja. It is a cattle fair. In which a large number of cattle are bought and sold. Other important festivals here are Holi, Basant Panchami, Dussehra, Durga Puja and Diwali.

Chhath is the biggest festival for the people of Bihar, Jharkhand and Eastern Uttar Pradesh. In this festival celebrated for 4 days, religious places, There is an increase in the beauty of rivers and ponds. Particularly in Bihar and Jharkhand, there is a special pomp of this festival. The worship of Lord Bhaskar is considered to be of special importance in the great festival of Chhath. Arghya is offered to the rising and setting sun on Chhath. In some cities of Jharkhand, Chhath festival is celebrated with all beliefs and traditions at special religious places.

Karma is a major festival of Jharkhand, Bihar, Odisha, West Bengal and Chhattisgarh. Mainly this festival Bhado It is celebrated on the day of Ekadashi of the month (approximately September) and in some places around it. On this occasion, people worship nature and pray for a good harvest, as well as sisters pray for the safety of their brothers.

Ram Navami Festival takes place in the month of March. Especially in northern India, this day is celebrated with pomp. New to Ayodhya, the city of Ram Like a new bride, she is decorated with flowers, bulbs, and colorful threads. After which Ram Navami procession is taken out. In which the chariot of Shri Ram is decorated a lot. In the chariot, the idol of the devotee Hanuman is kept along with Shri Ram along with his wife Sita, brother Lakshmana.

Sarhul is the main festival of tribals. After autumn, when the trees and plants turn green, the Sarhul festival begins. This festival lasts for a month. Every year Sarhul begins with the sighting of the moon on the third day of Krishna Paksha of Chaitra month.

Tusu festival is the most important festival of Kudmi and tribals of Jharkhand. It is celebrated in the month of Paush after the harvest in winters. The literal meaning of Tusu is virgin. There is no specific written source of the history of this festival. But there are many rituals in this festival and it is very colorful and full of life.
