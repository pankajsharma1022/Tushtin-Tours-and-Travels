The Indian state of Jammu and Kashmir is in different and different culture. It covers the different lifestyles and habits of the citizens living in the regions of Ladakh, Jammu and Kashmir. From an era of isolation, independence and unity, the citizens of Kashmir have created a distinctive tradition. The Kashmir Valley, which passes through the range of snow-clad Himalayas, is therefore considered one of the stunning destinations of the world. In addition Kashmir has the vast alluvial smoothness of a prosperous nation, glistening rivers, arrogant and hostile mountains, fast-moving waters, gushing ravines, dense Chinar orchards, large lakes and pine forests. Apart from this, Jammu and Kashmir is considered to be a dissolved water of diverse cultures. Citizens of different social practices and religious people are silent in this peaceful state. Since Jannat, various religions like Jainism, Islam and Hinduism have added additional value to the rich heritage of Jammu and Kashmir. So different tribes follow different religions and live in harmony. All these different types of religious classes have also contributed to the rich edifying tradition of Jammu. There is also an influence of Roman Mysticism and Greek Romanticism and this is evident in the Jammu due to the existence of Persian. The soulful colors of rustic living and fervor in Jammu and Kashmir, gives a clear sense of the citizens and traditions of Kashmir and the state of Jammu. A variety of festivals and fairs have made the life of men more enthusiastic. Starting from Eid-ul-Joh to Durga Puja, the citizens of Jammu and Kashmir enjoy with enthusiasm all the festivals and events. These festive examples interact with a lot of culture, people and lifestyle.
