The society of Uttarakhand means lifestyle and customs and traditions of the state. It is a heterogeneous mix of different ethnic groups from Kumaon and Garhwal regions. Most of the people belonging to the state society are heretics – Hindus and Buddhists; Whereas the Sikh state, which migrated from West Punjab and settled in Uttarakhand since 1947 The inhabitants here have been associated with life and human existence in the dance area; They showcase countless human emotions. Music forms an integral part of the culture of Uttarakhand. Popular categories of folk songs comprised of Basanti, Mangalas, Khuded and Chowpatty. The local craft, wood carving is quite prominent. The Kumbh Mela, one of the major Hindu pilgrimages, takes place in Haridwar, Uttarakhand and is considered the largest religious gathering in the world. Other major festivals in the state are Ghee Sankranti, Bat Savitri, Khatarua, Phool Dei, Harela Mela, Nanda Devi Fair, etc. Garhwali language and Kumaoni are the two main regional languages, but the most spoken language is Hindi. Kumaoni and Garhwali dialects are spoken in the Garhwal and Kumaon region. Some tribal communities in the west and north speak Jaunsari and Bhotiya dialects. On the other hand, the urban population mostly speaks Hindi, which is an official language of Uttarakhand along with Sanskrit. The history of Uttarakhand sings the glorious past of the region. It has a long history of its origin and development with the main features of great emperors and empires like Kushanas, Kudinas, Kanishka, Samudragupta, Katuria, Palash, Chandras and Pavara. Talking about the history of Uttarakhand, this reference can be said to be found in many holy Hindu scriptures. But its history can be best understood through the history of Garhwal and Kumaon. The distinctive flora and fauna of the state include snow leopards, bharals, tigers, leopards and unusual shrubs and fauna. The Yamuna and the Ganges, the two largest rivers of the Republic of India, have their source in this state.
