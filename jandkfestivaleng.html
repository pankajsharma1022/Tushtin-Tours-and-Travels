Many cultural festivals are celebrated in Jammu and Kashmir, which is called the paradise of India, which display the culture and civilization here. The festive season here begins with Lohri which marks the end of the cold season. In this festival, young boys and girls here dance and sing for several hours around the fire. The colorful festival of Holi is mainly celebrated here. Where people apply colors to each other with love. Jammu is also a holy land. Jammu is crowded during Navratri. Devotees come from far and wide to Jammu to see Mata Vaishno Devi. Other festivals celebrated in J&K are Guru Ravi Das Jayanti, Navroz, Eid, Diwali, Mahavir Jayanti and Buddha Jayanti.

Gurej Utsav is the major attraction of Gurez Valley. If you want to see the natural shade scattered somewhere in Jammu and Kashmir, then you must definitely go to Gurez Valley. If you want to visit the valley of Gurez So the best time to visit it is from May to October. Here you can also visit the Dargah of Baba Darwaish and Baba Razak and Pir Baba.

Hemis Festival is celebrated as the birthday of Padmasambhava. During this festival, a lama named Cham, adorned with masks with long horns, dances. And by playing special types of drums, Manjira and Siren, they perform religious drama. Mask dance is performed in various monasteries every year during this festival.

Like the gondola boat of Venice, these shikaras are considered a cultural symbol of Jammu and Kashmir. Shikara Festival The Shikaras of Alleppey are small in size and can accommodate 4 to 20 people. During the summer season, Shikara Festival is also organized in Dal Lake.

Tulip Festival: Official Spring Festival The Tulip Festival is a widely celebrated flower festival around the world. And why wouldn't it? These flowers, which spread their very fragrant fragrance in the air for some time and bloom for 15 to 20 days, Blooming looks even more beautiful.
