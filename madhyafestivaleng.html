The festivals of Madhya Pradesh are an integral part of the rich and diverse culture of the state. Many festivals are celebrated in Madhya Pradesh. Bhagoria Festival, an important tribal festival, is marked by traditional gaiety and enthusiasm. Shivratri is celebrated in Khajuraho, Bhojpur, Pachmarhi and Ujjain and has its own local flavor while the festival of Ram Navami in Chitrakoot and the princely state of Orchha has a unique spirit of devotion with tradition. The festivals of Orchha, Malwa and Pachmarhi bring forth the rich culture and art of the people of Madhya Pradesh. The long religious festivals of Hindu, Muslim and other communities are famous in Madhya Pradesh. All these fairs and festivals connect the spirits of different religions and paint them in the colors of celebration.

Lokrang Festival: Throughout the festival, the stage performs various forms of folk dances, music and ethnic dances. Lokrang is a cultural festival in which all the dancers put their top foot forward and mix their steps with the folk music. It is organized in Bhopal.

Khajuraho Dance Festival is an expression of proper Indian dance forms. All concerts take place in an open air stage right in front of the Chitragupta and Vishwanath temples. In this week-long cultural festival, a variety of dances are performed, which have their ancestry in India.

Bhagoria Haat Festival is one of its category in which young girls and boys have the right to run away after choosing their life partner. It is celebrated with great grandeur and performance by the tribal people of Madhya Pradesh.

Ujjain Kumbh Mel a honors the victory of good in the struggle for superiority against evil. This festival is organized four times in every twelve years in four cities of India- Allahabad, Haridwar, Ujjain or Nashik.

Malwa festival is one of the major and most amazing events in Madhya Pradesh. Artists from different parts of the world come to Indore and Ujjain to be a part of this wonderful festival of art, music, dance, drama and culture.

Pachmarhi Utsav: In this festival, for five days, a cultural evening is organized every evening, in which artists from national and international debates participate. The festival also has several stalls that serve delicious food. Inside the exhibition, the items on display are carpets, bamboo, dress materials and specially made sculptures by the local people.

Nimar Utsav: This is a three-day event full of music, dance, drama, boating and canoeing. It is organized by the state government of Madhya Pradesh and is celebrated on the banks of the holy river Narmada.

Chetiyagiri Vihara Festival: During the Chethiyagiri Vihara Festival in Sanchi people can witness the huge moment of Buddhist monks and followers. People come here to see the object of the two great disciples of Buddha, Sariputta, and Mahamogalasena.
