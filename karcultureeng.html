Karnataka is a southwestern state of India. It is bordered by the Arabian Sea to the west, and Telangana to the northeast. Tamil Nadu is in the east, Tamil Nadu in the south-east and Kerala in the south. It was originally known as Mysore State but in 1973 its name was changed to Karnataka. Bangalore or Bengaluru is the capital of India and a major IT hub or Silicon Valley. It is the 4th largest technology cluster in the world and ranks 1st digital city in the world. The state is also known as the knowledge, research and innovation center of Asia. With its splendid art and culture, Karnataka has a plethora of historical mysteries, amidst a rich and diverse culture It is a major tourist attraction with cities like Bangalore, the beautiful Coorg, the lesser known hill town of Chikmagalur, and heritage sites like Hampi, which attract countless visitors every year. Karnataka offers something for everyone, Be it a history lover, nature lover, or a person immersing themselves in their cultural heritage. It is home to various tribes, the unique Siddi community, numerous Tibetan refugees, the anthropological stigma and Kodava community, and others, who contribute to its unique culture. Art and Culture of Karnataka Karnataka - Culture and Tradition Karnataka art Earlier, painting involved the whole process, not just acting, In which there was a role from seeing someone's colors to coming into their life. Paper, wood, cloth, etc. were some of the materials artists used as the basis for their paintings. The brushes were not made from synthetic materials but from the native hair of animals such as camels, goats and squirrels. The Mysuru style of paintings usually represents legends, mythological scenes and the royal family. This style of painting from Karnataka is known across the country for its simplicity and intricate details. The artists used a special gesso paste, consisting of zinc oxide and glue, which formed a protective layer on the paintings, which has kept them unpainted even after 150 years. Karnataka is home to 50 different tribes, each with their own traditions and customs. Tribal art is another indigenous art. Hasi Chitra clay painting is an art form originating from Shimoga and Karwar districts And it is currently being revived.
