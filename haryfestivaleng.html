Haryana, the northern state of India, celebrates the festival with its own style. Haryana is similar to its neighboring states Delhi and Punjab. Celebrates its festivals. The festivals here are celebrated with enthusiasm, zeal and gaiety. People here come out on the streets to celebrate various festivals of all communities. Mainly the festivals of Diwali, Dussehra, Teej, Holi and Rakshabandhan are celebrated here.

Baisakhi means the festival of Vaishakh month. This Vaishakh is the first day of the solar month. Baisakhi is a defect of Vaisakhi. Bathing in the Ganges river is of great importance on this day. A huge fair is held in Haridwar and Rishikesh on Baisakhi festival. On the day of Baisakhi, the Sun transits into Aries. For this reason this day is also called Aries Sankranti. This festival is also called Equinox Sankranti. Baisakhi is traditionally celebrated on 13 or 14 April every year. This festival is important for Hindus, Buddhists and Sikhs. On the first day of Vaishakh, many New Year festivals like Jud Sheetal, Pohela Bosakh, Bohag Bihu, Vishu, Puthandu are celebrated in many regions of the entire Indian subcontinent.

Gita Jayanti was celebrated on 8th December last year. In Kurukshetra, Haryana, the main event of the International Gita Mahotsav was organized under the leadership of Chief Minister Khattar. Before the Ekadashi of the month of Margashirsh, Khattar and the CM and Governor of Uttarakhand started it with Gita worship and Yagya. In the Mango Festival mango varieties are displayed, as well as people related to mangoes come, competitions are organized. This festival has been organized since 1987. The 2006 festival was inaugurated by the Chief Minister of Delhi, Mrs. Sheila Dikshit. It was organized by Delhi Tourism and Traffic Development Department.

Pinjore Heritage Festival: Pinjore Heritage Festival is organized every year in the month of December at 'Pinjore Garden' And this festival is organized to support the rich culture of Haryana with its historical tradition.

Surajkund Mela is a colorful traditional craft festival of India, organized by the Government of Haryana. This fair was organized for the first time in the year 1987. The special thing about Surajkund Mela is that it is celebrated in different states of the country. Handicrafts and handicrafts made by artisans can be bought.
