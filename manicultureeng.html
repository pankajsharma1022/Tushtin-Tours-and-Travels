Polo and hockey are popular sports here. Manipur has also given birth to Manipuri, a local style of classical dance. Unlike other dance forms of India, this dance form uses hand postures rather than silent performances, the accents cannot be produced by the ghungroos, and the dancers and dancers perform this dance together. Dance plays, which are interpreted by the narrator, form an important part of the religious life here. The themes of these plays are generally taken from the life of Krishna, the animal husbandry deity of Hinduism. This dance, almost unknown for a long time, was introduced to the rest of India by the great poet Rabindranath Tagore in 1917. Raas, sankirtana dance and thang-ta (mridang playing) are also important parts of their cultural life. Apart from this, Manipuris are good warriors and practice wrestling, fencing and martial arts. They also play polo. Kaina is a place of pilgrimage for Hindus. Kaibul Lamjao National Park is the only floating sanctuary of its kind in the country. Other notable tourist places in the state include the ancient palace, Shri Govindji Temple of Vaishnavites, graveyards of war martyrs, the State Museum near the Polo Maidan, Langataband Mahal and Khwairamband Bazaar. This women's market is also called Nupi Keithal or Ima Market. About 91,000 tourists visit the state every year. There are many tourist places in this state. 7 KM from Imphal on National Highway 39. Far away is the Khongampat Orchid Garden, where more than 110 varieties of this flowering plant are available. 106 km from the state capital. Far away at an altitude of 1,757 meters is the beautiful hill station Mao. It is situated on the way between Imphal and Dimapur. Just 27 kms from Imphal. There is a temple of Vishnu in distant Bishnupur. Apart from this, Mohra is the place where Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose had hoisted the Indian tricolor on the land of India for the first time. This place is 45 km from Imphal. To the south is the Azad Hind Sena Memorial Building and the War Museum. The place is also famous for the ancient Manipuri folk culture. The nearby Loktak Lake offers magnificent views and there is also a deer park nearby. Tourist 165 kms.
