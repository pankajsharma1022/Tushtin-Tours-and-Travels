Gujarat, the western state of India, is known for its sweet speech and cuisine as well as for its colorful festivals. Many religious festivals are celebrated here with enthusiasm. The festivals here promote the spirit of unity and brotherhood. The festivals of Gujarat are extremely colorful and elaborate. The most colorful and popular festival here is the Navratri festival which takes place in the month of October. For nine nights, Mother Durga is celebrated as Aradhya Maan. In this festival people of all age groups gather in the temple premises and from morning They sing and dance for hours. Garba dance of Gujarat is famous all over the world. This festival ends with Dussehra. Other festivals celebrated in Gujarat are Diwali, Holi, Janmashtami and Raksha Bandhan International Kite Festival, Modhera Dance Festival are celebrated here in a grand manner.

Modhera Dance Festival attracts national and international tourists every year. The Sun Temple of Modhera is a popular tourist destination in the state. It is a definite delight to witness the celebration which is a reminder of the glorious past of this place.

Navratri is a major festival of Hindus. The word Navratri is a Sanskrit word, which means 'nine nights'. During these nine nights and ten days, nine forms of Shakti/Devi are worshipped. The tenth day is famous as Dussehra. Navratri comes four times in a year. It is celebrated from Pratipada to Navami in the month of Paush, Chaitra, Ashadh and Ashwin. Nine forms of three goddesses - Mahalakshmi, Mahasaraswati or Saraswati and Mahakali are worshiped in the nine nights of Navratri. Whose names and places are as follows Nanda Devi Yogmaya (Vindhyavasini Shaktipeeth), Raktdantika (Sathoor), Shakambhari (Saharanpur Shaktipeeth), Durga (Kashi), Bhima (Pinjore) and Bhramari (Bhramaramba Shaktipeeth) are called Navdurga. Navratri is an important major festival which is celebrated all over India with great enthusiasm.

Rann Utsav is organized in the desert of Kutch. Kutch Carnival is celebrated in December in Kutch district of North Gujarat. Which is also known as Rann Utsav. Camel ride can be enjoyed during the Rann Utsav organized on the Indo-Pakistan border. Thousands of people participate in the Rann Utsav held every year.

The celebration of the festival of Rath Yatra has started from Jagannath Puri itself. After this this festival started being celebrated all over India. Regarding Rath Yatra, it is believed that on this day a person gets salvation by helping in pulling the chariot. This is the reason why on this day devotees are eager to pull the chariot of Lord Balabhadra, Subhadra ji and Lord Jagannath.

Uttarayan is celebrated as Makar Sankranti in Gujarat. The first day is celebrated on 14 January and is called Uttarayan. The day of Uttarayan is celebrated by flying kites. Kite flying competition is organized across the state and people participate in kite fight.
