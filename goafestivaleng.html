Goa, is situated on the western coast of the Indian peninsula. Goa province is famous for its natural beauty and unique culture. before independence The province has been a colony of the Portuguese and the French. Because of this, even today, the full influence of western culture is visible on the living, language and food here. Although many festivals are celebrated in Goa, but in Goa, Christmas and New Year are celebrated with great pomp. Goa Carnival is a festive season in Goa which falls just before Lent; The main events usually take place during February. Carnival usually includes a public ceremony or parade In which elements of circus, masks and public open parties are performed. During the ceremony, people often adorn themselves or become bahuroopiyas, which reflects the rebound of daily life. Carnival is a festival traditionally held in Roman Catholicism. Goa has many such festivals. Which is celebrated only in Goa. Chikkal Kalo is one such festival. This festival is celebrated on the 12th day of Ashadh month. According to English daily it falls in the second or third week of July. If seen according to the season, it comes in the middle of the main season of Goa, the rainy season. It is a very enthusiastic festival of monsoon. Along with this, Diwali and Holi festivals are also celebrated with great enthusiasm in Goa.

The Bondarum festival is celebrated in the state in August. The charming and serene Divar Island comes alive with enthusiasm and fervor during this festival. And there is a carnival atmosphere that reaches to the mainland of Goa with the melody of the music.

The group of festivals that take place in Goa in the month of February is called Carnival. Carnival is a Portuguese word. Sao Joao Festival is the famous festival of Goa. This is one such festival celebrated on the beautiful land of Goa. Which is celebrated every year on 24th June with great enthusiasm. Because on this day the Catholic community pays respect to St. John the Baptist.

Shigmo or Shigmotsava festival is 9th as per Hindu calendar in the month of Phalguna (March). It is observed from moon day to full moon. It is the biggest festival for Hindus in Goa. On the first day, the village deity is bathed and dressed in saffron colored clothes. The 5th day is called 'Rang Panchami'.
