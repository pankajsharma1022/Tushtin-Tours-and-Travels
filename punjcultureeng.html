Folk songs, dances of love and war, fairs and festivals, dance, music and literature characterize the cultural life of this state. The origins of Punjabi literature can be traced back to the mystic and religious couplets of the 13th century Muslim Sufi saint Sheikh Farid and the Sikh cult. The founder can be linked to Guru Nanak of the 15th-16th century. Who used the Punjabi language extensively for the first time as a medium of poetic expression. The role of Waris Shah in enriching Punjabi literature in the second half of the 18th century is incomparable. At the beginning of the 20th century, the poet and writing began with the writings of Bhai Vir Singh and poets Puran Singh and Dhani Ram Chaitrik. Punjabi literature entered the modern period. Various writers portraying different aspects of Punjabi culture in recent years, The role of poets and novelists is also very important. The most famous of the poets were Mohan Singh Mahir and Shiv Kumar Batalvi; Notable among the novelists are Jaswat Singh Kanwal, Gurdial Singh and Sohan Singh Sheetal; Kulwant Singh Virk is a noted writer. One of the best novels depicting the rural life of Punjab is Mera Pind by Giani Gurdit Singh, a masterpiece of Punjabi literature. Many religious and seasonal festivals, such as Dussehra, Deepawali, Baisakhi and the anniversaries of various gurus and saints are celebrated in Punjab. Bhangra, Jhumar and Sammi are the popular dances here. Gidda, a local dance form of Punjab, is a humorous song-dance style of women. Along with Sikh religious music, ecclesiastical Mughal styles such as khayal, thumri, ghazal and qawwali are also popular. Among the notable architectural monuments of the state is the Golden Temple (Harmandir) located in Amritsar, which is built in the post-Mughal style. Prominences such as its dome and geometric motif are repeated in most places of worship of the Sikhs. gold zardozi work in golden temple, There are marble walls lined with embossed panels and colored stones. Other important buildings at Jallianwala Bagh in Amritsar The Martyrs Memorial, the Hindu Temple of Durgiana (also in Amritsar), the Moor-style mosque at Kapurthala and the old forts at Bhatinda and Bahadurgarh.
