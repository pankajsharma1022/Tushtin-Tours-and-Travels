The festivals of Maharashtra are a true reflection of the culture and tradition of the state and all its multicolored customs. Maharashtra is a state where a millennium culture weaves a tapestry of innumerable grains. For the people of Maharashtra who are considered to have a very lively nature, all the festivals are celebrated with gusto and enthusiasm. The Maharashtrian calendar is dotted with a year-long procession of festivals. His love for the festive season is well portrayed through various festivals.

The patron deity of the state of Maharashtra, Lord Ganesha is worshiped on the occasion of Ganesh Chaturthi. Ganesh Chaturthi is celebrated with great enthusiasm across the state and the eleven-day festival begins with the installation of beautifully crafted Ganesha idols in ghar mandapas' or large tents, which are beautifully decorated depicting religious themes.

Nag Panchami is celebrated in the honor of 'Nag Devta' and is one of the most important festivals in Maharashtra. As the worship of the serpent deity becomes an important ritual of the residents of Maharashtra, the earthen symbol of the cobra is worshiped in homes. The area of ​​Sangli, Maharashtra is famous for snake catchers.

Gokul Ashtami shows the actual occurrence of festivals in Maharashtra. Gokul Ashtami marks the birth of Lord Krishna. Spicy rice and curd is known as 'Gopal Kala'. A fun-filled ritual is performed on this day, known as 'Dahi Handi', in which earthen pots filled with curd, puffed rice and milk are placed high on the streets. A group of enthusiastic young men and women form human pyramids and try to reach and break them.

Banganga Festival is organized as a musical tribute to Lord Rama and aims to protect the rich cultural heritage of this Indian state. This festival is organized every year in the month of January at Banganga. It is in this popular festival that many talented artists from all over the country give their scintillating cultural performances before the enthusiastic crowd.

Kalidas Samaroh is celebrated in the month of November in Ramtek area of ​​Nagpur, in honor of the famous Sanskrit poet and playwright Kalidasa. Talented theater artists, dancers and musicians perform in this part of the country remembering their rich art and heritage.

Narali Purnima is celebrated as the full moon day of the month of Shravan according to the Hindu calendar. The festival is named because of people offering coconut to the ocean god on this day. The festival marks the arrival of the new fishing season and the charm of the ocean god, before the fishermen set out on the folk-beautiful boats. The festival of brothers and sisters, Raksha Bandhan is also celebrated on the same day.

Gudi Padwa usually celebrated by Maharashtrians as Hindu New Year celebrationIt is also considered as one of the most auspicious days as per Hindu traditions.

Pola Festival is a harvest festival and is celebrated all over the state of Maharashtra especially in rural areas. On this special day the bull, considered an integral part of the business of agriculture, is worshiped with great reverence and dedication; the farmers participate with great enthusiasm and enthusiasm.
